Scenario B

You received an email that looks like it's from your bank. The email says there is a problem with your account that needs fixing right away. You notice:

  • The email has spelling mistakes
  • It calls you "Dear Customer" instead of using your name
  • The sender's email is "support@bankaccount-secure.net" (not your bank's real website)
  • There's a button that says "Fix Account Now"
You clicked the link without thinking. The link took you to a fake website that looks like your bank's login page. You realized something wasn't right before entering your password, so you closed the website without giving any personal information. Now you want to report this.
